--- layout: archive title: "Research" permalink: /research/ author_profile: true --- I’m interested in a wide range of research topics around the themes of astrophysics and space science. My research is primarily centered on stellar structure and evolution, galactic archaeology, and time-domain astrophysics, utilizing observational techniques, theoretical models, and statistical analysis. Below I briefly summarize the major aspects of my research so far, along with representative outputs.

Galactic archaeology

As a member of Prof. Keith Hawkins' group, I am currently working on determining stellar abundances of phosphorus (P) in different parts of the Milky Way. For that purpose, I will employ spectroscopic data collected from the IGNRINS Spectral Archive and the HPF Instrument on Hobby-Eberly Telescope (HET). Apart from shedding light the production sites of P, this research aims to constrain the possible existence of life as in different galactic environments, since P is a bio-essential element.

Thermoelectric effect in neutron star crusts

This research, conducted along with Prof. Konstantinos N. Gourgouliatos, explores the impact of thermoelectric effects on the evolution of magnetic fields in neutron star crusts. By examining large temperature gradients associated with long-lived hotspots, we simulate the interplay between the Biermann battery mechanism, Hall drift, and Ohmic dissipation. Our findings reveal that significant magnetic field amplification occurs near temperature gradients, with fields reaching up to 1015 G for crust temperatures around 109 K. This study underscores the importance of thermoelectric effects in understanding neutron star magnetism, especially in stars with moderate-strength magnetic fields.

Photometry of potential planetary nebulae and symbiotic stars

During my internship at the National Observatory of Athens, advised by Dr. Stavros Akras, I processed data on low-ionization structures in planetary nebulae and analyzed multi-band photometric data to classify candidates as planetary nebulae or symbiotic stars.

Exoplanet TTV analysis through the ExoClock Project

I led the Literature Group of the ExoClock Project (coordinated by Dr. Angelos Tsiaras and Anastasia Kokori), focusing on updating exoplanet ephemerides for the Ariel mission. My work involved a fundamental Transit Timing Variation (TTV) analysis of promising exoplanets, contributing to our understanding of exoplanetary systems and their orbital dynamics.

Saraswati supercluster

As part of the ASIAA Summer Student Program, I worked with Dr. ​​​​Rogério Monteiro-Oliveira on data analysis of the Saraswati supercluster. This involved mapping luminous matter, assessing cluster spatial segregation by dynamical state, and examining the gravitational binding of the central region using SDSS and HSC Survey data. These efforts have significant implications for the understanding of large-scale structure in the universe.
